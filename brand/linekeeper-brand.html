The Fusion

Two marks, one identity

Neither name changes what the app is — but together they name it exactly. Touchline gave the vantage and the tracking ring. Keeper gave the trust and the clean-sheet check. The overlap is literal: touch·line ends where keeper begins.

Why it works as one word

  • It's a real -keeper.Sits beside goalkeeper, bookkeeper, scorekeeper, timekeeper. The suffix already means “the one who keeps it straight.”
  • Every line, kept.The touchline you manage from, the goal line you defend, the bottom line you balance, the roster line by line. One word holds all four.
  • Ownable — no collision.“Keeper” alone bumps into a password manager. “Linekeeper” is coined and clear; the name is yours.
  • Survives Spanish.Reads as a proper noun, meaning carried by the mark — not an English-only pun the way “in touch” was.

Lineage of the tagline

  • Touchline said:“Run the whole club from the touchline.” — the vantage.
  • Keeper said:“Keep a clean sheet.” — the trust.
  • Linekeeper says:“Keep the line.” — hold the defensive line, hold the bottom line, in one breath.

The Mark

The tracking ring, closed by a check

One continuous move: the open ring is the club being tracked and run; the check is the season closed and the books reconciled. The clay pentagon at its heart is the ball — carried straight over from Touchline. It reads the same on a splash screen or a 16 px tab.
