The Name

Why “Keeper”

The goalkeeper is the one position a team trusts with the result — the last line, the one who protects what everyone worked for. This app puts that same guardian in front of a club's money, roster, and schedule. And a keeper is also a bookkeeper: the one who keeps the records straight.

The case

  • The club's most trusted position.The keeper guards the result on the pitch; the treasurer and manager guard it off it. Same job, one name.
  • Keeper = bookkeeper.One who keeps: the ledger, the roster, the documents, the season. Kept — not lost in a spreadsheet.
  • A clean sheet, on and off the pitch.In soccer a clean sheet is a match with nothing conceded. In the books it's every dollar reconciled. Same standard, one word.
  • One word, two languages.Reads as a proper noun in English and Spanish. The meaning lives in the role and the mark — not an English-only pun the way “Touchline / in touch” did.
